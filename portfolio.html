The Living Arks

Complete custom crafted ecosystems. Magical refuges for endangered species. Living art for sacred spaces.

All plants are propagated in cultivation or wild collected with permits for ex situ conservation. Hardscape, plants, and mosses are ethically sourced with provenance.
The Madagascan Ark
The Madagascan Ark
29.5" tall x 13.5" wide Barcelona recycled glass floor lamp
Established: March 7th, 2026
Begonia lyallii • Begonia bogneri • Selaginella madagascariensis • Angraecum rutenbergianum • Angraecum urschianum • Microcoelia aphylla • Asplenium sp. Madagascar
Region: Andringitra region, Central Highlands, Madagascar
Elevation: 800–1500 meters
Habitat: Humid montane forests
Hardscape: Madagascan Gneiss
This Ark preserves a fragment of Madagascar's vanishing cloud forests. It is home to Begonia lyallii and Begonia bogneri, two threatened Madagascar endemic begonias. Selaginella madagascariensis, a spikemoss found only on the island, carpets the forest floor. Angraecum rutenbergianum and Angraecum urschianum, both Madagascar endemic orchids, climb alongside Microcoelia aphylla, a leafless orchid found from Kenya to South Africa and Madagascar. Asplenium sp. Madagascar, an undescribed species of spleenwort, unfurls among the mosses.

A prayer for one of the world's most threatened biodiversity hotspots.
Permanent Collection | Not for sale
The Bornean Cloche Ark
The Bornean Cloche Ark
12.5" tall x 8.5" wide converted cloche desk lamp with hand embroidered lampshade
Established: February 16th, 2026
Sonerila sp. Holographica • Begonia sp. Sarawak • Begonia lichenora • Selaginella sp. Blue Borneo
Region: Sarawak, Malaysian Borneo
Elevation: 300–1000 meters
Habitat: Lowland to montane rainforests, limestone hills
Hardscape: Bornean limestone
A miniature limestone hill ecosystem, home to some of the world's most specialized and endangered Begonias. Sonerila sp. Holographica, Begonia sp. Sarawak, and Begonia lichenora are all Borneo endemics, the first two undescribed by science. Selaginella sp. Blue Borneo, an iridescent blue form found only on the island, thrives in the humidity of this cloche.

A charm for biodiversity in a landscape threatened by mining and deforestation.
Permanent Collection | Not for sale
The New Guinea Cloche Ark
The New Guinea Cloche Ark
12.5" tall x 8.5" wide converted cloche desk lamp with burlap lampshade
Established: April 18th, 2026
Bulbophyllum cruciatum • Cadetia umbellata • Piper sp. Timika • Hypnum plumaeforme • Rhynchostegiella papuensis
Region: Finisterre Range, New Guinea (spanning Papua New Guinea and West Papua, Indonesia)
Elevation: 1000–2000 meters
Habitat: Montane cloud forests
Hardscape: Finisterre Lava Rock
This Ark holds a fragment of the Finisterre Range, home to one of the most biologically diverse and threatened forests on Earth. Bulbophyllum cruciatum, a New Guinea endemic, shares space with Cadetia umbellata, a miniature orchid found from Maluku to New Guinea. Piper sp. Timika is an undescribed species from Papua, Indonesia. Mosses include Hypnum plumaeforme and Rhynchostegiella papuensis, the latter found only in New Guinea.

Here, orchids grow on every branch and mosses drape the trees like veils.
Permanent Collection | Not for sale

Orchidarium Arcula
Small epiphytic terrariums featuring 12 countries, 27 species of threatened and vulnerable orchids, with 13 species of mosses and liverworts, including authentic regional hardscape. This collection represents six months of planning. All hardscape, plants, and mosses are ethically sourced with provenance.
